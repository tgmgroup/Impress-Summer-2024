Dai 2's Favorite Sweets

In the summer, people love to eat sweets and candies. They are very popular at festivals during this time of year. Most of these sweets and candies you can find at your local convenience store. In the gallery are some of Dai 2 Junior High School’s favorite sweets.

Some candies and sweets are well known in Japan and America, like Haribo gummies, Oreos, and KitKats. However, in Japan, there are matcha flavored KitKats, while in America, there is only a chocolate flavor.

What do the students say about sweets?

Seiji says, “I like donuts because they are very soft.”

Rino says, “I like strawberry chocolates because they are delicious.”

Krisha says, “I like pancakes because they are sweet.”

How about you? What do you think about sweets?

Hometown Sweets

These days, we can get sweets from other countries in Japan, such as halo-halo from the Philippines. But there are still many sweets that we can’t get here.

Bannister misses a lot of sweets. He really likes the sweet and unique Ju-Mui Pops from Hawaii.

David misses licorice. It has a powerful taste, so “you need time to love it,” but some Japanese people say “it tastes like tires.”

Alice likes pufuleti, or Romanian corn puffs. Her husband said, “They’re so bad. Give me more!”

Melinda likes pastillas de leche, or milk candies. They are milky and chewy.

The world is full of delicious things. If you get the chance, please travel around the world and try some of them!

Parfaits

Parfaits are originally from France (In French, parfait means "perfect"). French parfaits are like custard cakes and are made from sugar, eggs, and cream. American parfaits are made from layers of ice cream, fruit, and other delicious fillings.

Chocolate Pretzels

Pretzels are salty ropes of bread, but chocolate pretzels are crunchy biscuits under a candy shell. They were first made in Hamburg, Germany, by Herr Franz Joseph Liebniz in 1544.

Chupa Chups

Chupa Chups are a brand of lollipops from Spain. Enric Bernat, their creator, put caramel on a stick to help kids keep their hands clean while eating.

Crepes

In Japan, crepes are sweet, but in France, where they were first made, crepes can be sweet or savory (salty or spicy and with meat or vegetables).

Ju-Mui Pops

Ju-Mui Pops are from Bannister's home state of Hawaii in the USA. Ju-Mui Pops are a mix of Chinese and American culture. They have li hing mui, or hoshiume (like umeboshi) inside a fruit juice popsicle.

Licorice

Licorice is a candy made from the licorice plant. It tastes a little like medicine and you should not eat too much of it. David likes it, but the flavor is strong and the candy feels a little like rubber. Originally from England, most licorice candy is sweet, but some are salty. There is also red licorice, which does not contain any licorice plant, and comes in many fruit flavors.

2 / 7 (15)

Pastillas de Leche

Pastillas de Leche are a very chewy milk candy from the Philippines, where Melinda is from. The first pastillas de leche were made from carabao (water buffalo) milk and calamansi (the Philippine lemon) juice. They are made by boiling the milk with sugar and lemon juice for a very long time. Now there are soft and hard versions of pastillas with many added flavors, including durian and peanut.

Reese's Peanut Butter Cups

Reese's Peanut Butter Cups are a chocolate candy from the USA. Sweet peanut butter is put in a chocolate cup. It's delicious. Ms. Tsubuko from Sakai Kita Junior High says it's perfect with a cup of black coffee. They're one of David's (and George's) favorite candies.

4 / 7 (15)

Willy Wonka's Everlasting Gobstoppers

Willy Wonka's Everlasting Gobstoppers are a candy created by the great British author Roald Dahl in Charlie and the Chocolate Factory. It's a huge hard candy with many layers of flavors that takes a long, long time to eat, and the purpose of the candy is "to stop a mouth (from talking)." In the USA, they're known as jawbreakers because they are so big and hard. They're one of Bannister's favorite candies.

Pufuleti

Pufuleti are crunchy puffed corn snacks from Alice's home country of Romania. Many countries have their own corn snacks, including Fritos in the USA and Tohato's Caramel Corn in Japan. When Alice's husband first ate pufuleti, he said, "まずい！もっとちょうだい。"

6 / 7 (15)

Cherry Ripe Bars

Cherry Ripe bars are made of cherry-flavored coconut filling coated in dark chocolate. They are from Ryan's home country of Australia. They were first made in 1924, one hundred years ago!
