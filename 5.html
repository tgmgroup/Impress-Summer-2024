The Sweetest Teacher

There are many opportunities to communicate with friends in English class. One of the benefits and fun aspects of being in it is that you can ask questions, listen to performances, talk to friends, and get to know them better.

I also think that through English lessons, we learn the meaning and origin of words that we didn't know in the past. Furthermore, it is good to gain an understanding of another country's language and culture.

Though I'm still not very good at English, I like it. I want to be able to speak it more. I recently started watching Korean drama with fascination that the thought of learning Korean even crossed my mind. Truly, we have an opportunity to  speak the language that we have learned, and it feels good to use it to communicate even only to some degree. I'm looking forward to having a fun English learning activity with all of you.

Dreams of American Children

What do students in the USA want to be when they grow up?

This survey of 1,000 students was conducted in 2022 by Ellie's Sparkle Show

0 / 10 (23)

1. Doctor/Nurse/Healthcare Professional (26.2%)

Healthcare professionals help people stay healthy and get better when they're sick. They include doctors, nurses, and others who check on your health, treat illnesses, and give advice on staying healthy in both mind and body. They work in places like hospitals, clinics, and doctor's offices. It's tough, but these jobs are needed a lot.

1 / 10 (23)

2. Teacher (16.5%)

Teachers help students learn new things. They explain subjects like math, reading, and science, and make sure students understand. They also help with homework, answer questions, and support students in their studies.

2 / 10 (23)

3. Scientist (12.0%)

Scientists study how things work in the world. They ask questions, do experiments, and look for answers to learn more about things and solve problems. Some famous scientists include Albert Einstein, Marie Curie, Isaac Newton, and Charles Darwin.

3 / 10 (23)

4. TikToker / YouTuber / Vlogger (11.9%)

TikTokers, YouTubers, and Vloggers create and share photos and videos. They often dance, sing, or do funny skits. They might make videos about gaming, cooking, vlogs, or tutorials. They often share about their daily lives, travel, fashion, or fitness. Some famous TikTokers, YouTubers, and Instagrammers are Zach King (magic tricks), MrBeast (big challenges and giveaways), Kim Kardashian (fashion).

4 / 10 (23)

5. Actor (10.9%)

Actors perform in movies, TV shows, or plays. Some famous actors are Tom Cruise (Mission: Impossible), Leonardo DiCaprio (Titanic), and Zendaya (Spider-Man).

5 / 10 (23)

6. Police Officer (9.7%)

Police officers help keep people safe and enforce the law. They patrol neighborhoods, respond to emergencies, and investigate crimes. They also help people in trouble and make sure everyone follows the rules.

6 / 10 (23)

7. Dancer (9.7%)

Dancers perform different types of dance, like ballet, hip-hop, or contemporary, to entertain or tell a story. They practice a lot to perfect their moves and often perform on stage, in music videos, or in competitions. Some famous dancers are Charli D'Amelio, a TikTok star, JoJo Siwa, who started on Dance Moms, and Kyle Hanagami, who creates dance routines for popular songs.

7 / 10 (23)

8. Veterinarian (9.1%)

Veterinarians are doctors who take care of animals. They help pets like dogs and cats, as well as farm and zoo animals, by checking their health, giving them medicine, and performing surgeries when needed.

8 / 10 (23)

9. Dentist or Dental Hygienist (6.7%)

Dentists are doctors who take care of your teeth and mouth. They fix problems like cavities, clean teeth, and check for any issues.

Dental hygienists clean your teeth and check for any signs of problems. They also teach you how to brush and floss your teeth properly.

Dreams of Japanese Children

What do junior high school students in Japan want to be when they grow up?

This 2024 survey of 657 students in Japan was conducted by Kuraray.

0 / 10 (23)

1. Athlete (8.8%)

Athletes train hard, but it's fun to play the sports you love. Some famous athletes are LeBron James, a basketball player, Simone Biles, a gymnast, Naomi Osaka, a tennis player, Abe Hifumi, a judoka, and Yui Kamiji, a wheelchair tennis player.

1 / 10 (23)

2. Doctor or Nurse (5.3%)

Doctors and nurses help people stay healthy and treat them when they are sick. They also give advice on how to prevent illnesses and manage your health. One famous doctor from Japan is Dr. Shinya Yamanaka, who won the Nobel Prize for his work in stem cell research.

2 / 10 (23)

3. Teacher (4.9%)

Teachers help students learn new things. They explain subjects, give assignments, and help with problems. They also support students in their studies and encourage them to do their best.

3 / 10 (23)

4. Animator or Illustrator (4.7%)

Animators make moving pictures for movies, TV shows, or video games. Illustrators draw pictures for books, magazines, and other things. Some famous Japanese examples are Hayao Miyazaki (of Studio Ghibli) and Hiromu Arakawa (Full Metal Alchemist).

4 / 10 (23)

5. Scientist (3.8%)

Scientists study the world around us to understand how things work. They ask questions, do experiments, and look for answers to solve problems and improve our lives. One famous Japanese scientist is Hideki Yukawa, Japan's first Nobel Prize winner.

5 / 10 (23)

6. Pharmacist (3.0%)

Pharmacists help people by giving out medicine and explaining how to use it safely. They make sure the medicines are correct and can offer advice on how to take them and any side effects.

6 / 10 (23)

7. Zookeeper or Theme Park Worker (3.0%)

Zookeepers take care of animals in zoos. They also help with animal training and educate visitors about the animals.

Theme Park Workers help guests at amusement parks and theme parks. They make sure that everyone has a fun and safe experience.

7 / 10 (23)

8. Patisserie (3.0%)

Pastry Chefs make delicious baked goods like cakes, cookies, and pastries. They mix ingredients, bake treats, and decorate them to make them look and taste great.

8 / 10 (23)

9. IT Engineer (2.9%)

IT Engineers keep our computer systems running and build internet infrastructure.

9 / 10 (23)

10. Healthcare Worker (2.7%)

Healthcare workers include doctors, nurses, and social workers.
