The World's Hardest Desserts

Ice cream. Meringueメレンゲ: a kind of food made from whipped egg whites. Cake. Each of these foods is delicious. Put them together, and it becomes Baked Alaska, an incredible素晴らしい: great, amazing dessert first made in 1867. That’s the year the USA bought Alaska from Russia.

To make Baked Alaska, you start with a layer of sponge cake. You put ice cream on top. You whip泡立てる: to beat, to mix egg whites and sugar into meringue and put that all around your ice cream and cake. Then you bake that in a very hot oven for five minutes. If you do it right, you’ll have an amazing combination of hot, crispyサクサク: crunchy (like in good fried chicken) meringue, fluffyふわふわ: light, full of air (like bread) cake, and cold ice cream.

It sounds easy, but Baked Alaska is actually one of the most difficult desserts to make in the world. The cake can be too dry乾いた: having no water or too wet濡れた: full of water. The meringue can be too soft. And the ice cream melts溶ける: like when ice becomes water very quickly! It’s very easy to make a sweet but disappointing残念な: something that makes you sad mess失敗作 (台無し、乱雑): something that is difficult or dirty when you make Baked Alaska. This is why it is a challenge for bakers of all skills.

The world is full of difficult desserts, including baklava, pavlova, macaroons, soufflés, baumkuchen, and croquembouche. What do you think? Would you like to try making some of these difficult, delicious desserts?

Baked Alaska Challenge: Let's try making Baked Alaska!

Ingredients

1. 1.5 kg of ice cream

2. 450 grams of pound cake

3. 6 egg whites

4. 150 grams of sugar

5. 4 mL of cream of tartar


Read the whole recipe at SpendWithPennies!

Instructions

1. Cut pound cake into 3-cm thick slices. Put the slices into a large bowl. Freeze the bowl and cake.

2. Scoop some ice cream onto the cake in the bowl. Push down on the ice cream so it sticks on the cake. Freeze it again.

3. Put the egg whites and cream of tartar in a bowl. Beat it with a mixer for about three minutes. Add sugar little by little and beat until it gets a little hard.

4. Cover the ice cream and pound cake with meringue. Freeze it again.

5. Bake the Baked Alaska in a hot oven at 260 degrees Celsius for 3 to 5 minutes. Take it out when the meringue is light brown.

6. Let it rest for 5 minutes, then cut it and enjoy! If you did it right, it will be crispy, creamy, hot, cold, and delicious.
